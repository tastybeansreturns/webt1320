The Joys of Fishing

My mom wishes I would do something more "productive" with my spare time, but I always tell her that fishing builds my body, mind, and soul.

The Body

For me, a typical fishing excursion consists of at least 4 hours of continuous walking and standing, rain or shine. When it shines, I get all that vitamin D goodness.

The Mind

When it rains I get wet, which I dislike; if you don't like something, it must be good for you; therefore getting wet builds my character.

The Soul

The dopamine released from fishing cannot be matched by any earthly substance. Hooking into a biggin' gets my heart racing faster than when I do cardio. The pure satisfaction of finessing a fish and then catching it is of the purest, most unadulterated joys.

The Woes of Fishing

I'd be lying if I said I wish I could always go fishing. It gets pretty darn expensive - very easy to lose $20+ in equipment on a single fishing trip. Fishing line gets tangled and lures get stuck (irretrivable). Falling into algae infested water isn't much fun either. After a particularly bad day (ie. no fish caught, lots of lures lost) I don't feel like fishing for the next week.

The Biggest Woe of All

No one has experienced true pain until they lose a whopper of a fish. The regret is real. I always remember the fish that got away more than the fish I catch. It's even worse when the bugbites from earlier in the day kick in on the forlorn drive home. And yes, fishing is much much more fun when fish are caught.
